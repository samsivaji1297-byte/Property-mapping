# 🧰 Kitchen Tap Aerator — Field Notes / Reference Guide

## What an Aerator Is
- A small component at the end of a tap spout.
- Regulates water flow, pressure, and spray pattern.
- Adds air to the stream to reduce splash and save water.
- Consists of:
  - **Housing** (metal ring that screws onto the tap)
  - **Insert / Cartridge** (plastic internal piece — the part that actually regulates flow)

---

## Symptoms of a Missing or Damaged Aerator
- Tap outputs **too much water**
- Water stream becomes **chaotic** or **splash-heavy**
- Pressure feels unregulated
- Flow is louder than usual
- Sometimes leaks or sideways spray

---

## Why It Happens
- The insert falls out
- The housing loosens
- The insert gets clogged and someone removes it
- Wear and tear over time

---

## How to Identify the Part
The insert typically has:
- A **grid pattern** on one side (water diffuser)
- A **star-shaped key pattern** on the other (for installation/removal)
- Standard sizes: **22mm** or **24mm**
- Thread types:
  - **Male aerator** → screws *into* a female-thread tap
  - **Female aerator** → screws *onto* a male-thread tap

---

## Quick Tap Thread Check
Look at the end of the tap spout:

- **Threads on the outside** → tap is male → you need a **female aerator**
- **Threads on the inside** → tap is female → you need a **male aerator**

---

## What to Buy at Bunnings
You can buy:
- **Full aerator kits** (housing + insert)
- **Insert-only replacements** (less common but available)

Recommended approach:
- Buy **both sizes** (standard + small)
- Buy **both thread types** if unsure
- Low cost, high convenience, zero future friction

---

## Installation Steps
1. Unscrew old housing (if present).
2. Insert new cartridge into housing (if needed).
3. Screw housing back onto tap.
4. Hand-tighten only — no tools required.
5. Turn tap on to confirm:
   - smooth flow
   - no leaks
   - no sideways spray

If spray is uneven → remove, rinse, reinstall.
